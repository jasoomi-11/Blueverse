Story / Plot

The Odyssey (2026) is an upcoming epic adventure film based on Homer’s legendary Greek poem. Directed by Francis Lawrence, the visionary filmmaker behind The Hunger Games series and I Am Legend, this adaptation promises to bring ancient mythology to life with modern cinematic grandeur.

The story follows Odysseus (Ralph Fiennes), the cunning king of Ithaca, as he embarks on a perilous ten-year journey home following the fall of Troy. Battling divine wrath, mythical creatures, and his own inner turmoil, Odysseus must fight to reunite with his wife Penelope (Daisy Edgar-Jones) and their son Telemachus.

Produced by Lionsgate and Temple Hill Entertainment, the film is said to combine practical sets with advanced CGI to capture Greece’s breathtaking landscapes, treacherous seas, and encounters with gods like Poseidon and Athena. Its screenplay — written by Patrick Ness — focuses on the emotional and psychological depth of Odysseus’s odyssey, portraying both heroism and human fragility.

The Odyssey aims to revive the grand mythological genre for a new generation, mixing the emotional scale of Gladiator with the visual wonder of Dune. With a budget exceeding $180 million, it’s expected to be one of the most ambitious and visually stunning releases of 2026.
