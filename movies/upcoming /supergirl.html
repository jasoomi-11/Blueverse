Story / Plot

Supergirl (2026) is an upcoming American superhero film based on the DC Comics character Kara Zor-El, cousin of Superman. The film will be part of the first chapter of James Gunn’s rebooted DC Universe, titled "Gods and Monsters". Directed by Craig Gillespie, the movie will follow Kara’s journey as she grows up on a fragment of Krypton that survived its destruction, leading to a much darker and more isolated upbringing compared to her cousin Kal-El.

Unlike previous portrayals, this version of Supergirl is inspired by the 2022 comic series "Supergirl: Woman of Tomorrow" written by Tom King. The film will explore Kara’s emotional depth and resilience as she sets out on a cosmic journey of justice, loss, and identity. It is expected to have a more mature tone than past DC films, reflecting the complex storytelling approach of the new DCU under James Gunn and Peter Safran.

The film will connect to Superman: Legacy (2025) and establish Supergirl’s place in the larger shared universe. Milly Alcock, best known for her role in HBO’s House of the Dragon, stars as Supergirl, marking her debut in the superhero genre. The film’s production began under DC Studios and Warner Bros. Pictures, with cinematography and world-building aligning with Gunn’s overarching DCU vision.

With high anticipation from fans and critics, Supergirl aims to reimagine one of DC’s most beloved heroes through a grounded yet powerful narrative, balancing cosmic adventure and emotional storytelling.
