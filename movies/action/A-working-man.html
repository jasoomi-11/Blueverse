Story / Plot

A Working Man (2025) follows Jack Doyle, a blue-collar mechanic and former soldier trying to keep his head down after years of violence. When his small-town garage becomes entangled in a money-laundering scheme connected to a ruthless cartel, Jack is pulled back into a world he thought he’d left behind.

As federal agents close in and dangerous enforcers hunt him, Jack must fight to protect his family and uncover who set him up. Every decision draws him deeper into a web of corruption that reaches from the streets of his hometown to powerful corporate interests hiding in plain sight.

Forced to rely on his old combat instincts, Jack turns from an ordinary laborer into an unwilling vigilante, using the very skills he swore never to use again. The story explores themes of redemption, loyalty, and survival — showing how one man’s fight for peace can ignite a storm he can’t control.

Directed by David Ayer, A Working Man blends tense drama with gritty action, grounded in emotional realism and raw performances. With Jason Statham leading a powerhouse cast, the film combines moral conflict and hard-hitting sequences in a story about the cost of doing what’s right when the world plays dirty.

From dimly lit workshops to high-stakes city pursuits, the film paints a portrait of a man defined not just by the violence in his past, but by his determination to build a better future — no matter the cost.
