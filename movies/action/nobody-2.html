Story / Plot

Nobody 2 (2025) continues the story of Hutch Mansell, the seemingly ordinary family man whose violent past as a government assassin was revealed in the first film. After dismantling a criminal empire in the suburbs, Hutch is trying to rebuild a quiet life with his family—until his old world finds him again. When a mysterious international syndicate targets him and his loved ones, Hutch is forced out of retirement for one more ruthless mission.

As the sequel opens, Hutch’s attempts at normalcy crumble when an encrypted signal alerts him that several of his former targets have resurfaced. What begins as a simple act of self-defense spirals into a globe-spanning vendetta, pitting Hutch against assassins, mercenaries, and a powerful figure from his past who knows every detail of his methods.

With the help of his father David and brother Harry, Hutch sets out to dismantle the syndicate from within—using the same brutal precision and dark humor that made him a legend in the underworld. The sequel deepens Hutch’s character, exploring the cost of violence, the illusion of control, and the unshakable pull of chaos.

From neon-lit safe houses in Eastern Europe to high-speed chases through city streets, Nobody 2 raises the stakes with relentless action and explosive choreography. Every fight pushes Hutch closer to confronting who he truly is: a man born for the fight, trying to convince himself he’s done with it.

Directed by Timo Tjahjanto and written by Derek Kolstad, the film promises a darker, more emotional continuation of the first movie’s blend of black comedy and bone-crunching combat. With stylish visuals, brutal efficiency, and Bob Odenkirk returning at his sharpest, Nobody 2 is an adrenaline-fueled evolution of the modern action antihero story.
